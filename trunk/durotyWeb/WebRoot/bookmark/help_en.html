How do I use Advanced Search?

Advanced search operators are query words or symbols that perform special actions in Duroty search. These operators allow you to find what you're looking for quickly and accurately.

Operator Definition Example(s)
title: Used to specify the bookmark title Example - title:amy
Meaning - Bookmarks with title Amy
url: Used to specify a bookmark:url Example - url:duroty.com
keywords: Search for words in the keywords field Example - keywords:dinner
contents: Search for words in the bookmark contents Example - contents:dinner
comments: Search for words in the comments Example - comments:project
OR Search for bookmarks matching term A or term B*
*OR must be in all caps 		Example - title:amy OR title:david
Meaning - Bookmarks title Amy or title David
-
(hyphen) 		Used to exclude bookmarks from your search Example - dinner -movie
" "
(quotes)
 Used to search for an exact phrase*
*Capitalization isn't taken into consideration 		Example - "i'm feeling lucky"
Meaning - Bookmarks containing the phrase "i'm feeling lucky" or "I'm feeling lucky"
( )
Used to group words
Used to specify terms that shouldn't be excluded 		Example - title:amy(dinner OR movie)
Meaning - Bookmarks from Amy that contain either the word "dinner" or the word "movie"
